Usage notes

[edit]
\n

P\u00e5 only means at when it cannot mean on top, usually when talking about abstract concepts (which extends to clubs, hospitals, and other places where the focus is normally not on the building itself) rather than physical objects that one may be on top of. For at in a truly spatial sense, use vid. See also the usage notes for inuti.\n

In the time sense, p\u00e5 is only used for speaking of days when describing them in some sense: p\u00e5 julafton, p\u00e5 min f\u00f6delsedag, p\u00e5 m\u00e5ndag; when giving a date this preposition is excluded: Han k\u00f6pte bilen den tredje maj = He bought the car on the third of May.\n

The sense of can also be expressed using the genitive. For example, the phrase \u00f6gat p\u00e5 n\u00e5len above is interchangeable with n\u00e5lens \u00f6ga.\n

Schools work most similarly to American English. \"I skolan\" (in the school) is idiomatic for attending school as a student. Other people, including teachers (l\u00e4rare), would be \"p\u00e5 skolan,\" though you could also talk about \"l\u00e4rare i skolan\" (teachers in the school), where \"skolan\" refers to the entire school system (compare svenska (\u201cSwedish\u201d), k\u00e4rlek (\u201clove\u201d), and m\u00e4nniska (\u201chuman\u201d) in the definite). Students might also be \"p\u00e5 skolan,\" either when not currently attending school or when focusing on the location. \"To go to school\" as in being a student is expressed as \"att g\u00e5 i skolan\" (to go in the school). Compare the \"l\u00e4rare i skolan\" example. A person inside the school building would be \"inne p\u00e5 skolan\" or \"inne i skolan\" (see the usage notes for inuti), where \"inne i skolan\" focuses solely on the building and not its purpose. See also duktig for a school-related usage example.\n
