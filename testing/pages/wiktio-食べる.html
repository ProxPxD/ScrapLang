Etymology

[edit]
\n

From earlier Old Japanese form \u98df\u3076 (tabu, \u201cto (humbly) receive or get\u201d, from a superior), a verb with the \u4e0b\u4e8c\u6bb5 (shimo nidan) or lower bigrade conjugation pattern. This was the humble form of \u8cdc\u3076, \u7d66\u3076 (tabu, \u201cto (deign to) give\u201d, to an inferior), a verb with the full \u56db\u6bb5 (yodan) or quadrigrade conjugation pattern.[1][2]\n

\n
\n
Development
\n
\n

Natural progression from Old and Classical Japanese \u4e0b(\u3057\u3082)\u4e8c(\u306b)\u6bb5(\u3060\u3093)\u6d3b(\u304b\u3064)\u7528(\u3088\u3046) (shimo nidan katsuy\u014d, \u201clower bigrade conjugation\u201d) verbs ending in -u to modern Japanese \u4e0b(\u3057\u3082)\u4e00(\u3044\u3061)\u6bb5(\u3060\u3093)\u6d3b(\u304b\u3064)\u7528(\u3088\u3046) (shimo ichidan katsuy\u014d, \u201clower monograde conjugation\u201d) verbs ending in -eru. \n

\n
\n

Most likely cognate with \u7d66\u3046 (tamau, \u201cto give\u201d, as a superior gives a thing to, or does something for, an inferior).\n

Compare the historical development of meaning from \u201cto humbly receive\u201d \u2192 \u201cto eat\u201d, with the modern use of the humble verb \u9802\u304f (itadaku, \u201cto receive\u201d) just before eating, in a sense of \u201cI humbly receive this food.\u201d\n

Verb

[edit]
\n

\u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u308b \u2022 (taberu) transitive ichidan (stem \u98df(\u305f)\u3079 (tabe), past \u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u305f (tabeta))\n

\n
  1. to eat\n
    \u3054\u98ef(\u306f\u3093)\u3092\u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u308b
    go-han o taberu
    to eat a meal
    \n
    \u7bb8(\u306f\u3057)\u3067\u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u308b
    hashi de taberu
    to eat with chopsticks
    \n
    \u4eca\u65e5(\u304d\u3087\u3046)\u306f\u3001\u5bff(\u3059)\u53f8(\u3057)\u3092\u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u306b\u9280(\u304e\u3093)\u5ea7(\u3056)\u306b\u884c(\u3044)\u304d\u307e\u3059\u3002
    Ky\u014d wa, sushi o tabe ni Ginza ni ikimasu.
    I'll go to Ginza today to eat sushi.
    \n
    \u98df(\u305f)\u3079\u3066\u3044\u304f\u305f\u3081\u306b
    tabeteiku tame ni
    in order to make a living (lit. in order to keep eating)
    2. \n
  2. (archaic, humble) to eat or drink\n
    • 1603, Vocabvlario da Lingoa de Iapam (Nippo Jisho) [Vocabulary of the Language of Japan] (in Portuguese), Nagasaki, page 594:
      Tabe, uru, eta. \u30bf\u30d9, \u30d6\u30eb, \u30d9\u30bf (\u98df\u3079, \u3076\u308b, \u3079\u305f) \u98df\u3046, \u307e\u305f\u306f, \u98f2\u3080.
